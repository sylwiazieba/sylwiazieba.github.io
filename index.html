From Run-Off to Regeneration

Data Analysis & Storytelling with ArcGIS StoryMaps & ArcGIS Pro

Entered and selected as Finalist in the 2024 ArcGIS StoryMaps Competition. This map-focused data storytelling explores agrochemical pollution from fertilizers and pesticides from US farming operations and its disproportionate effects on the health and safety of communities nearby and downstream of the Mississippi River. Climate solutions, particularly the current landscape and incentivization of regenerative agriculture practices, are discussed.

Digging Up the Energy Transition

Exploratory and Geospatial Data Analysis with Python

As the world embraces a transition to clean energy, supply will need to grow exponentially by 2030 due to demand from new technologies like electrical vehicle (EV) and battery production. This piece explores hazardous labor conditions from artisinal mining sites in the Eastern DRC, a major exporter of cobalt and other critical minerals, made from data made available by International Peace Information Service (IPIS). It is further overlayed with World Protected Areas (WPA) data to analyze the potential environmental disruption that surrounding protected natural areas are facing.

Powering Climate-Resilient Communities with Solar Energy

Solar Suitability Analysis with Remote Sensing Data and Building Footprints

Renewable energy lends itself to local and decentralized production and distribution that can help mitigate fossil fuel-related emissions, improve air quality, and empower local cities to make decisions that are truly in the best interest of their communities' needs. A case study focused on Allentown, Pennsylvania using LiDAR from USGS 3DEP LidarExplorer and building footprints from Pennsylvania Spatial Data Access to conduct a rooftop suitability analysis to estimate solar power potential.

Analyzing Forest Loss and Agricultural Expansion in Laos

Evaluating Land Cover and Vegetation Change using Landsat imagery in ArcGIS Pro

Laos, one of Southeast Asia's last remaining expanses of primary forest, is seeing an increase in forest loss due to increasing demand for its agricultural products. Using satellite data and GIS analysis, the article explores where forest cover has diminished while cropland and shifting cultivation have increased. It also discusses the role of local communities and government policies in forest restoration, highlighting both challenges and successes in managing Laos' natural resources.
